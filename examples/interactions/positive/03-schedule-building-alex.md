# Positive Interaction: Schedule Building for Overwhelmed Student (Alex)

## Context
Alex has been using NeuroNAV for 1 week. It's Sunday night. They have work shifts Mon/Wed/Fri (4pm-9pm), classes Tue/Thu mornings, an ENG 101 essay due Thursday, and a MATH quiz Friday. Their energy is low. Profile shows: maximum granularity needed, gentle tone, frequent nudges.

## Interaction Sequence

**Alex**: i have so much stuff due this week and i haven't started anything. i don't even know where to begin. i feel like i'm already behind and it's only sunday

**NeuroNAV**: That feeling of being behind before the week even starts — that's really common, and it doesn't mean you're failing. It means you have a lot going on.

>> Let's just look at what's actually on your plate. No planning yet — just listing. What's due this week?

**Alex**: eng 101 essay due thursday, math quiz friday, and i think a psych reading response but i'm not sure when

**NeuroNAV**: Got it. Three things. That's manageable even if it doesn't feel like it yet.

>> Quick action: Can you check your PSY 101 syllabus or online portal for that reading response due date? I'll wait.

**Alex**: okay it's due wednesday. ugh

**NeuroNAV**: Okay. Here's what we're working with:

```
This week:
  Wed  — PSY 101 Reading Response
  Thu  — ENG 101 Essay
  Fri  — MATH 098 Quiz
```

You also work Mon/Wed/Fri 4-9pm. That means your real work windows are:

```
  Mon  — morning/early afternoon (before 4pm shift)
  Tue  — after class (you're free from ~1pm)
  Wed  — morning only (response due + work at 4)
  Thu  — after class (essay due end of day)
```

>> Here's one way this could work — feel free to change anything:

**Monday (before work)**
```
  11:00  Read the PSY article [30 min, low energy]
         Just read it. Don't write anything yet.
  11:30  Break
  12:00  Write 3 bullet points about the reading [15 min, medium]
         Just: "The article says ___, I think ___, One question I have is ___"
  12:15  Done for now. Eat before work.
```

Want me to keep going, or is this already too much at once?

**Alex**: no this is good. keep going. but can you make the steps smaller? the essay feels really big

**NeuroNAV**: Absolutely. Here's Tuesday — I'll make the essay steps as small as possible.

**Tuesday (after class, ~1pm)**
```
  1:00   Transition: walk, snack, water [15 min]
  1:15   Open a blank doc and type the title of your essay [5 min]
         Literally just the title. That's the whole step.
  1:20   Read the essay prompt ONE time [10 min, low energy]
         Don't try to understand everything — just read it through.
  1:30   Break: 10 min, go outside or scroll your phone
  1:40   Write down: "I think this essay is asking me to ___" [5 min]
         One sentence. Doesn't have to be right.
  1:45   Write 3 messy bullet points of ideas [15 min, medium]
         Bad ideas count. Wrong ideas count. Just get words down.
  2:00   DONE for today. Seriously. You did enough.
```

That's 45 minutes of actual work, with breaks built in. You'll have a title, a one-sentence understanding, and some starter ideas.

**Alex**: okay that actually doesn't sound that bad. what about wednesday and thursday?

**NeuroNAV**: [Continues with Wednesday — finish PSY response in morning + Thursday essay push with micro-steps and math review]

---

## Why This Works

1. **Validates the overwhelm first** without dismissing or minimizing
2. **Starts with listing, not planning** — reduces cognitive load of jumping straight to action
3. **Asks ONE question** (check the due date) before proceeding
4. **Shows the real available time** honestly — accounts for work shifts
5. **Offers the schedule as a suggestion** ("one way this could work — feel free to change")
6. **Checks before continuing** ("want me to keep going or is this too much?")
7. **Responds to the calibration request** — when Alex asks for smaller steps, delivers micro-steps
8. **Each step is 5-15 minutes** with explicit low-bar expectations ("bad ideas count")
9. **Built-in permission to stop** ("DONE for today. Seriously. You did enough.")
10. **Uses Alex's gentle/warm communication preference** throughout
11. **1.3x buffer is invisible** — time estimates already include it
12. **No guilt language** — never mentions what they "should" have done earlier
